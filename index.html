This is what Board does — collect, organize, rediscover, tagged by AI that speaks your design language.

You save hundreds of images. Then you never find them again.

Pinterest is built for everyone. Bookmarks turn to chaos. Desktop folders are invisible to the tools you actually design in. Between collecting inspiration and starting real work, there's a gap — and nothing lives there. Board does.

01 — CAPTURE

Save any image on the web without leaving the page.

A Chrome extension that right-click-saves or hover-saves any image, anywhere — Dezeen, Instagram, a supplier site, a competitor's portfolio. One click, zero decisions. The image goes straight to your library; the details get handled later.

  • Right-click or hover-save on any website
  • iOS share extension for saving on mobile
  • Auto-assign to your active project board
02 — INTELLIGENCE

AI tagging that speaks your design language.

Every save is analyzed by Claude and tagged with discipline-specific vocabulary. You don't get "orange wall." You get terracotta accent wall, mid-century modern, warm earth palette. Board tags the aesthetic, not the object — 100+ nuanced color names, mood, era, and material, all searchable.

  • Discipline-aware: interiors, UI, or fashion vocabulary
  • Color names like burnt orange — never just "orange"
  • Mood, style era, and material, tagged automatically

03 — ORGANIZE

A visual-first home for everything you've ever saved.

Your entire library as a masonry grid. Filter by AI tags, colors, and moods. A discovery strip surfaces the aesthetic patterns you reach for most. Flip any card to reveal the full tag breakdown, the color palette with hex codes, and the original source.

  • Filter by color, mood, and style instantly
  • Card flip → palette, hex codes, source attribution
  • Project boards with auto-assign on save
See it where you work

Your inspiration, inside the tools you design in.

Board doesn't ask you to switch contexts. Your library lives inside Figma, SketchUp, and Canva — search it and drop references straight onto the canvas.
